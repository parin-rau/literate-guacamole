Mechanical Engineering Projects

Overview

  • Roll-out solar array designed in-house to mount to satellites
    • Like tape measure covered in flexible solar cells
  • Significantly cheaper than traditional Z-fold rigid panel solar arrays
  • Less than 6 months to go from almost-completed prototype in the lab to shipping first fully space-qualified solar array system
  • Deployable Mechanisms/Actuators
    • Hold-Down Release Mechanisms (HDRMs) to react launch loads
    • Double-hinged yoke to deploy array away from spacecraft
    • Geared stepper motors to drive deployment of solar cell booms

Responsibilities

  • End-to-end design of deployment guide system
  • Support new product introduction of entire solar array system
    • Qualification/acceptance testing
    • manufacturing processes
    • Test fixture/tool design

Deployment Guide Development

  • Last major R&D hurdle before locking design for first flight
  • Like shape of opening on tape measure
  • Guides boom material from flat profile in coiled state to curved profile as it deploys
  • Crucial to functionality of solar array
    • Prevent buckling/crumpling of booms and damaging solar cells
    • Array pointing
    • Maintain stiffness under satellite maneuvering acceleration

Design Process

  • Rapid prototyping
    • Generate concept geometry in CAD
    • 3D print
    • Run full deployment test with roll of sheet metal on prototype
    • Iterate on design until booms no longer crumple during deployment testing
  • Analysis
    • Aluminum chosen for final design for strength and quick turnaround time for parts
    • Modal analysis to prevent coupling with modes of the rest of the solar array structure
    • Worst case quasi-static load of fully deployed boom weight applied to guides (fixed-fixed beam with center load)
  • Finalized design within allotted 1 month and placed order for machined aluminum flight design

Testing Issues

  • Booms (aluminum substrate) crumpling when testing deployment with finalized metallic design mounted to flight solar array system
  • PLA 3D printed plastic parts from prototyping phase had favorable friction properties vs aluminum finalized guides
  • Inserted PTFE/Teflon shims around guide-boom interfacing surfaces and solved crumpling issue on subsequent deployment test
    • Fixed friction problems at minimal weight cost
    • Material on-hand for quick fix to prevent schedule slip
    • Space-rated material, so no further redesign required

Test Fixture Design

  • Verify yoke hinges and HDRMs survived environmental qualification testing (thermal cycling, vibration)
  • Folding sidewall simulator to support static base structure of the solar array
    • COTS parts and plasma-cut steel plates for rapid turnaround time
    • Easy integration of TASSA to fixture
  • Air bearings (furniture movers) mount to TASSA to support deployer structure and booms over floor to simulate 0g environment for deployment
    • Time consuming fine tuning of air bearing presures because of uneven floor surface
    • Consider offloading from above in the future
  • After calibrating fixture, successfully verified HDRM and hinge mechanisms survived environmental tests and yoke umbilical harnesses (not pictured) have sufficient torque margin

Drivetrain system

Development

  • Linear actuator for retracting wheel away from track surface when not in use, and pushing wheel into track to maintain traction
  • Motor, gear, and wheel sized for moving vehicle at 5mph with minimal power and volume requirements
  • Bellcrank linkage design for compact physical footprint of overall system
    • Limited vertical space to position system in vehicle
    • Translates horizontal movement of linear actuator into vertical movement of wheel and motor
  • Design passed cricial design review (CDR) with SpaceX engineers judging overall vehicle design
  • Custom metallic parts designed for fabricating with waterjet for quick lead times
  • Actuator and motor driven by Arduino interfacing with vehicle's main onboard PLC
  • System performance demonstrated on fully-loaded vehicle

Boat

Overview

  • Aluminum heatsink to passively dissipate 500W heat generation in turbulent flow region in the rear of an EV race car
  • Interface control documents from motor controller vendor used to determine mounting points and expected heat generation from controller to the heatsink
  • Built in strain-relief bracket for high voltage cables connected to the motor controller
  • Comparative studies in Solidworks Flow with simulated wind tunnel to down select final heat sink fin grid layout
  • Created G-code for CNC milling the part from aluminum stock
  • Lap time improvements of several seconds because of improved motor controller cooling
